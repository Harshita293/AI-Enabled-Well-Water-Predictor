# Literature Survey

## Introduction
Groundwater and well water prediction has become an important research area due to water scarcity and climate change. Various machine learning techniques have been proposed to improve prediction accuracy.

---

## Paper 1
**Title:** Groundwater Level Prediction Using Machine Learning  
**Method:** Linear Regression  
**Findings:** ML models provide better prediction than traditional methods  
**Limitation:** Limited dataset

## Paper 2
**Title:** Well Water Level Forecasting Using Random Forest  
**Method:** Random Forest  
**Findings:** High accuracy for nonlinear data  
**Limitation:** Model complexity increases

## Paper 3
**Title:** Prediction of Groundwater Fluctuation Using ANN  
**Method:** Artificial Neural Networks  
**Findings:** ANN captures complex patterns  
**Limitation:** Requires large training data

## Paper 4
**Title:** A Study on Water Level Prediction Systems  
**Method:** Statistical + ML methods  
**Limitation:** Not automated

## Paper 5
**Title:** Machine Learning Based Groundwater Analysis  
**Method:** SVM  
**Limitation:** Less effective for large datasets

## Paper 6
**Title:** AI Techniques in Water Resource Management  
**Method:** Multiple ML models  
**Limitation:** Lack of web integration

## Paper 7
**Title:** Environmental Factor Based Water Prediction  
**Method:** Regression Techniques  
**Limitation:** Location specific

## Paper 8
**Title:** Smart Water Management Using AI  
**Method:** Hybrid ML approach  
**Limitation:** Expensive implementation

## Paper 9
**Title:** Groundwater Monitoring and Prediction  
**Method:** Time-series analysis  
**Limitation:** Requires long-term data

## Paper 10
**Title:** Survey on Groundwater Prediction Methods  
**Method:** Comparative study  
**Limitation:** No real-time prediction

---

## Research Gap
- Most existing systems lack a user-friendly web interface
- Limited focus on local well water prediction
- Integration of AI with software applications is minimal

## Proposed Solution
The proposed system uses machine learning with a web-based interface to predict well water availability efficiently and accurately.
